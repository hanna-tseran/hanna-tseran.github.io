I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in computer science working in Guido Montúfar's group at Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences (MPI MiS) in Leipzig, Germany. I am also a student at International Max Planck Research School for Mathematics in the Sciences (IMPRS MiS). I expect to graduate in $\color{teal}{\text{July 2023}}$. 

I am broadly interested in $\color{teal}{\text{machine learning}}$, focusing more on the $\color{teal}{\text{mathematical aspects}}$. I believe we should comprehend how the existing deep learning systems work to build more advanced ones. Hence, I am particularly fascinated by the $\color{teal}{\text{theory of deep neural networks}}$ for its benefits for understanding deep learning mechanics, knowledgeably designing machine learning models, and ensuring positive societal impacts.

I am curious about various topics in the field, but recently I have been incredibly excited by neural network $\color{teal}{\text{expressivity}}$, $\color{teal}{\text{generalization}}$, and $\color{teal}{\text{optimization}}$. Lately, I have been concentrating more on the effects of the $\color{teal}{\text{activation function}}$ and $\color{teal}{\text{network initialization}}$ on these questions and studying $\color{teal}{\text{over-parametrization}}$, $\color{teal}{\text{NTK}}$, and $\color{teal}{\text{implicit bias}}$. Additionally, I enjoy considering what it would take for the models to operate in the real world. With this intent, I studied $\color{teal}{\text{few-shot learning}}$, $\color{teal}{\text{continual learning}}$, and $\color{teal}{\text{long-term memory in conversational AI}}$. My other interest is practical architectures, such as $\color{teal}{\text{transformers}}$, $\color{teal}{\text{graph neural networks}}$, and $\color{teal}{\text{Bayesian neural networks}}$.
