I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in computer science working in Guido Montúfar's group at Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences (MPI MiS) in Leipzig, Germany. I am also a student at International Max Planck Research School for Mathematics in the Sciences (IMPRS MiS). I expect to graduate in <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{July 2023}}$</ins>. 

I am broadly interested in <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{machine learning}}$</ins>, focusing more on the <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{mathematical aspects}}$</ins>. I believe we should comprehend how the existing deep learning systems work to build more advanced ones. Hence, I am particularly fascinated by the <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{theory of deep neural networks}}$</ins> for its benefits for understanding deep learning mechanics, knowledgeably designing machine learning models, and ensuring positive societal impacts.

I am curious about various topics in the field, but recently I have been incredibly excited by neural network <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{expressivity}}$</ins>, <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{generalization}}$</ins>, and <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{optimization}}$</ins>. Lately, I have been concentrating more on the effects of the <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{activation function}}$</ins> and <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{network initialization}}$</ins> on these questions and studying <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{over-parametrization}}$</ins>, <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{NTK}}$</ins>, and <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{implicit bias}}$</ins>. Additionally, I enjoy considering what it would take for the models to operate in the real world. With this intent, I studied <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{few-shot learning}}$</ins>, <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{continual learning}}$</ins>, and <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{long-term memory in conversational AI}}$</ins>. My other interest is practical architectures, such as <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{transformers}}$</ins>, <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{graph neural networks}}$</ins>, and <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{Bayesian neural networks}}$</ins>.
