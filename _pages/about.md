I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in computer science working in Guido Montúfar's group at Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences (MPI MiS) in Leipzig, Germany. I am also a student at International Max Planck Research School for Mathematics in the Sciences (IMPRS MiS). I expect to graduate in <ins>$\color{teal}{\text{July 2023}}$</ins>. 

I am broadly interested in <ins>machine learning</ins>, focusing more on the <ins>mathematical aspects</ins>. I believe we should comprehend how the existing deep learning systems work to build more advanced ones. Hence, I am particularly fascinated by the <ins>theory of deep neural networks</ins> for its benefits for understanding deep learning mechanics, knowledgeably designing machine learning models, and ensuring positive societal impacts.

I am curious about various topics in the field, but recently I have been incredibly excited by neural network <ins>expressivity</ins>, <ins>generalization</ins>, and <ins>optimization</ins>. Lately, I have been concentrating more on the effects of the <ins>activation function</ins> and <ins>network initialization</ins> on these questions and studying <ins>over-parametrization</ins>, <ins>NTK</ins>, and <ins>implicit bias</ins>. Additionally, I enjoy considering what it would take for the models to operate in the real world. With this intent, I studied <ins>few-shot learning</ins>, <ins>continual learning</ins>, and <ins>long-term memory in conversational AI</ins>. My other interest is practical architectures, such as <ins>transformers</ins>, <ins>graph neural networks</ins>, and <ins>Bayesian neural networks</ins>.
